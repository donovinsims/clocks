## Section 4: My Approach

**Math Before Pitch.** Every client conversation starts with numbers, not promises. The ROI calculation is the prerequisite for every sales conversation. If the math doesn't work for you, I'll tell you that before you spend a dollar.

**Automation built for the truck, not the tech stack.** Most automation platforms are built for digital businesses. My clients run their operations by phone calls and texts. Every system I build fits those channels — because that's where the business already lives.

**Fixed price, full ownership, no retainer.** The business model is deliberately structured to make monthly retainers unnecessary. One-time pricing isn't a discount — it's the moat. The infrastructure costs fractions of a cent per transaction. There is no overhead to cover, so there is no recurring bill to justify.

**Specific leaks only.** I don't offer "AI transformation" or vague operational consulting. I fix missed calls, cold estimates, no-shows, weak reviews, and manual invoicing. Nothing else gets added to scope.

**Technology stays invisible.** Clients receive a one-page doc and a Loom handoff video. They never need to understand APIs, webhooks, or workflow builders. Complexity is my problem, not theirs.

**Local accountability.** Showing up in person, shaking hands, and standing behind every installation in the Roscoe/Rockford/Beloit corridor isn't a limitation. It's the moat. Remote agencies can't replicate it.

## Section 5: Brand Voice Characteristics

**1. Confidently Boring**
No hype. No dramatic promises. Every claim is backed by math, timelines, and dollar figures. The goal is to sound like the most reliable peer at the supply house — not a marketer.

**2. Ruthlessly Practical**
Every sentence serves a purpose: expose a leak, prove the math, or explain the fix. Filler is cut. Fluffy qualifiers are cut. If it doesn't move the reader toward clarity or action, it doesn't ship.

**3. Peer-to-Peer**
This is not an expert talking down to a client. It's one operator talking to another. Blame goes to the old way of doing things or to greedy agencies — never to the owner who's been working 70-hour weeks doing great work.

**4. Localized and Specific**
Vague claims don't build trust. Exact numbers do. "A Rockford HVAC company." "$1,200 recovered on day one." "68% of missed callers never call back." Specificity is authority.

**5. Math-First**
ROI before everything. The math is not a promise — it's a calculation done before money changes hands. When the numbers are clear, the sale is a logical conclusion, not a persuasion event.

**6. Zero Jargon**
Never "AI-powered transformation." Never "disruptive workflow optimization." Always "missed call text-back." Always "the system sends a text when your phone goes to voicemail." Complexity stays behind the curtain.

## Section 8: My Content Philosophy

1. **Expose the leak before offering the fix.** A solution without a quantified problem is a pitch. Show the math first. Let the reader feel the cost of inaction before they ever see a price.

2. **Every sentence must earn its place.** If it doesn't expose a leak, prove the math, or explain the fix — cut it. There is no room for filler in content read by exhausted operators scanning on their phones at 9 PM.

3. **Specificity is credibility.** Exact dollar amounts, exact geographies, exact percentages. "Significant revenue loss" means nothing. "68% of missed callers never call back, and the average ticket is $900" means everything.

4. **Shame belongs on the system, not the operator.** The reader has been working 70-hour weeks doing great craft work. The failure is the analog setup — not the person running it. Blame the old way. Respect the operator.

5. **The content is the product.** Every post, article, and email should deliver enough operational insight that a reader could act on it immediately. When they hit a wall and want it done for them, the Free Assessment is one click away.

## Section 12: Tone Guidelines

**Use:**

- Contractions (you're, it's, that's, they've)
- Exact dollar figures and percentages rather than approximations
- Local geography references (Rockford, Roscoe, Beloit, Winnebago County)
- Blue-collar/industrial metaphors (leaking bucket, plugging holes, installing a system)
- Short declarative sentences followed by math, followed by the fix
- Colons to introduce systems, steps, or calculations
- Numbered lists for every multi-step process
- Plain trade-specific language (dispatch, ticket, call-back, estimate, no-show)

**Avoid:**

- Jargon (leverage, synergy, revolutionize, disruptive, AI-powered transformation)
- Vague claims without dollar figures attached
- Emojis (they signal "marketing")
- Corporate capitalization (Synergy, Ecosystem, Transformation)
- Passive voice
- More than three sentences per paragraph
- Words that sound like they belong in a pitch deck

**Style:** Peer-to-peer. Confidently boring.

**Energy:** Direct. Grounded.
