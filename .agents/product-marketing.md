**One-liner:** Local-first automation for owner-operated service businesses. Flat fee. You own it.

**What it does:** Finds the exact operational leak costing the business money, builds a custom automation fix, installs it in 3–7 days. No retainer. No platform to log into. The system runs on your phone, your number, your existing tools.

**Product type:** Productized service — done-for-you automation installs

## 2. Target Audience

### Ideal Customer Profile

**The qualifier is not the industry or the headcount. It is the situation.**

If the owner is still the one answering calls, chasing leads, and doing admin at night — this is for them.

| Dimension          | Description                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Role**           | Owner-operator. Name is on the truck, the license, and the bank account. Does the craft work and runs the business simultaneously.                                                                     |
| **Location**       | Northern Illinois corridor — Rockford, Roscoe, Beloit (WI). Winnebago County, Boone County, Stephenson County, I-39 corridor. Local only.                                                              |
| **Industry**       | Any owner-operated local service business. Primary proof cases: HVAC, plumbing, electrical, auto repair, general contracting. Also: real estate, nail salons, barbershops, home services, landscaping. |
| **Team size**      | Any. Solo operator to 20-person crew. The bottleneck is the owner, not the headcount.                                                                                                                  |
| **Business stage** | Established and financially viable. Not a startup. Not struggling. The owner's work ethic grew the business. Now the business outgrew the owner's time.                                                |
| **Tech adoption**  | Late majority to skeptic. Has tried tools before and abandoned them. Hears "AI" as tech industry noise.                                                                                                |

### Jobs to Be Done

1. **Stop losing revenue to missed calls** — 68% of missed callers never call back. $800–$1,200 per missed call.
2. **Recover cold estimates automatically** — Sequenced follow-up at 48 hours, 7 days, 14 days. Closes 18–30% of cold bids.
3. **Rebook no-shows the same day** — Empty slots become revenue instead of lost time.
4. **Generate reviews without manual requests** — Text goes out the hour the job ends. Direct link to Google profile.
5. **Stop chasing invoices at 10 PM** — Polite automatic chase at 7, 14, and 21 days.
6. **Triage after-hours leads by urgency** — Emergencies ring through. Quotes wait. Owner stops reacting to noise.

### Use Cases (from site copy)

- Under a truck on Harrison Avenue. Phone rings, goes to voicemail. Before the caller closes the screen, they get a text: "Hey — sorry I missed you. I'm on a job. What can I help with?"
- Send a voice memo from the cab: "New estimate for Tom on Harrison, furnace, $3,200." The system writes the estimate, logs the lead, schedules follow-ups.
- Reply "yes" or "send it" via text. No dashboard. No app. No calendar to fight with.
- A missed call while on a roof → within 60 seconds the lead receives a text, a follow-up is scheduled, and the job is booked — all without the owner touching it.

- **Dread** — Working 70 hours a week and still watching profitable jobs walk to a competitor because a call went unanswered.

## 5. Competitive Landscape

### Direct Competitors

**Local marketing agencies and ops consultants**

- Charge $3,000–$8,000/month retainers
- Focus on traffic, brand awareness, and ad spend — not operational leaks
- Never show up on-site at the shop
- Client has nothing tangible to own at the end
- Require Slack channels, QBRs, monthly calls
- Clockout response: "No agency calling you on the 1st of the month."

### Secondary Competitors

**Vertical SaaS (Jobber, Housecall Pro, ServiceTitan, HubSpot, generic CRM)**

- Require changing operations to fit the software
- Dashboards that go unopened after week one
- Monthly subscription that never ends
- Learning curve for non-technical owners
- Clockout response: "Builds around what you already use. You don't change a thing."

**DIY automation platforms (Zapier, Make)**

- Steep learning curve
- No one to configure, test, or maintain
- Owner reverts to manual within weeks
- Clockout response: Done-for-you install. You text it. It does the work.

### Indirect Competitors

**Doing it manually**

- Owner is the system. The bottleneck is their time.
- 8–12 hours/week of after-hours admin
- Clockout response: "Get your evenings back."

**Hiring a part-time admin / virtual assistant**

- Ongoing salary cost ($20–$30/hr)
- Doesn't cover after-hours calls
- Requires management overhead
- Clockout response: One-time install. Runs 24/7. No management required.

### What the Market Calls This Category

The site never uses the category names the market would use ("AI automation," "CRM," "operations consulting"). It positions Clockout as:

- "Local automation operator"
- "Message-based assistant"
- "The fix" (not the software)
- "Local operator" (not agency, not SaaS)

## 8. Brand Voice

### Tone & Personality

| Attribute              | Definition                                                                                                              |
| ---------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Tone**               | Declarative, fact-based, direct. No hype. No fluff.                                                                     |
| **Personality**        | Peer at the supply house, not a tech founder. Hard-working, transparent, local, pragmatic. Anti-corporate. Anti-agency. |
| **Relation to reader** | Peer-to-peer, never expert-to-client. "I'm a local operator. I drive."                                                  |
| **Emotional register** | Empathetic to the trades. Blames the system and the industry, never the operator.                                       |

### Voice Rules (from CLAUDE.md — enforce these)

| Rule                     | Application                                                                                                                  |
| ------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Sentence length**      | 8–14 words average                                                                                                           |
| **Paragraph length**     | 1–3 sentences. One idea only.                                                                                                |
| **Vocabulary**           | 6th–8th grade. "Dispatch," "call-back," "estimate," "ticket," "no-show," "invoice." Not "synergy," "leverage," "streamline." |
| **Punctuation**          | Minimal commas. Heavy periods. Colons to introduce systems or cost breakdowns.                                               |
| **No corporate case**    | Never capitalize Synergy, Ecosystem, Transformation, Solutions, Optimization                                                 |
| **No emojis**            | Zero. Not in copy, not in CTAs.                                                                                              |
| **No exclamation marks** | None. Ever.                                                                                                                  |
| **Numbers over claims**  | "3 missed calls × $800 = $2,400 lost." Not "significant revenue loss."                                                       |
| **Proof before pitch**   | Show the operating cost before introducing the fix. Math calculator before pricing section.                                  |
| **Time/cost callouts**   | "Time to build: 3 days. Cost: $500 flat."                                                                                    |
| **No video language**    | Never "watch this" or reference any video. Copy and static layout only.                                                      |
| **No SaaS vibes**        | No "Supercharge your workflow." No feature grids.                                                                            |
| **Mobile-first**         | Short paragraphs. Phone-scanable.                                                                                            |
